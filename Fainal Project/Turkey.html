Hagia Sophia Grand Mosque

Originally built by the eastern Roman emperor Justinian I as the Christian cathedral of Constantinople for the state church of the Roman Empire between 532 and 537, and designed by the Greek geometers Isidore of Miletus and Anthemius of Tralles,[4] it was formally called the Church of the Holy Wisdom [5] and was then the world's largest interior space and among the first to employ a fully pendentive dome.

Fairy chimneys in Goreme

Cappadocia is famous for its wonderfully strange rock formations and its distinguished archaeological history. One of the best places to see these strange formations is the village of Goreme. The village is located in the middle of large numbers of conical porous stone known as “fairy chimneys” that were formed by wind and water erosion of two volcanic layers: a thick layer of porous stone covered with a thin layer of basalt that resists erosion. Many of these chimneys in Cappadocia were hollowed out over the centuries to create homes, churches, and storage facilities due to the ease of carving in the porous stone.
