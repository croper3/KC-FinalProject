History of Philosophy in Greece

Greek philosophy originated in the 6th century BC and continued through the Hellenistic period, and covered a wide range of subjects, including political philosophy, ethics, metaphysics, ontology, logic, biology, rhetoric, and aesthetics.

Spartans

Sparta or Sparta (Greek: Σπάρτη) (Latin: Sparta) is located on the side of the Eurotas River in the southeast of the Peloponnese. It emerged as a political entity around the 10th century BC, when it was conquered by the Dorians during 650 BC. Military dominant power in ancient Greece.

Athens

Athens (Greek: Αθήνα) is the capital and largest city of Greece. Athens dominates the region of Attica and is one of the oldest cities in the world, and its recorded history extends back to more than 3,400 years, and human presence begins in it between the eleventh and seventh millennium BC. The city's name goes back to Athena, the Greek goddess of wisdom. Athens is located in southern Greece on the Attica plain between the Ilissos and Kifissos rivers, surrounded on three sides by the peaks of the mountains Hemito (1,026 m), Pindeli (1,109 m), and Parnithas (1,413 m). It overlooks from the fourth side the Saronicus Gulf, which connects to the Mediterranean Sea.

Greco-Persian Wars

The Greco-Persian Wars (Persian: nghai, Iran and Greece) were a series of wars between the Achaemenid Empire and the Greek states that began in 499 BC and ended in 449 BC. The conflict between the Greeks and the Persians began when Cyrus the Great occupied the Greek region of Ionia in 547 BC. The Persians struggled to control the independent population of Ionia, so they appointed several tyrants to rule the cities in the region, which posed many problems for the Greeks and the Persians alike.
