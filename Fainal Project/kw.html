Battle of Raqqa

The Battle of Raqqa is a naval battle that took place on August 12, 1783 between Kuwait and the tribe of Banu Ka’b, rulers of Arabistan, which ended with the victory of Kuwait.

Kuwait's independence

The State of Kuwait became independent on Monday, 5 Muharram 1381 AH, corresponding to June 19, 1961 AD. This independence came after negotiations that began after the British High Commissioner in Kuwait informed the Emir of Kuwait, Sheikh Abdullah Al-Salem, of Britain’s intention to withdraw from Kuwait and grant it independence. In return, the Emir of Kuwait will be established Kuwait is a parliament with limited powers and a free or semi-free press. Preparations for the independence of Kuwait began in 1958 AD by issuing laws such as the Kuwaiti Nationality Law and other laws that create a sovereign state and an independent entity.
